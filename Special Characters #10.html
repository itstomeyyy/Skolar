Here are some special characters using in HTML entities:

  • & - Ampersand (Represents &)
  • < - Less than (Represents <)
  • > - Greater than (Represents >)
  • " - Double quote (Represents ")
  • ' - Single quote (Represents ')
  • © - Copyright symbol (Represents ©)
  • ® - Registered trademark symbol (Represents ®)
  • ™ - Trademark symbol (Represents ™)
  • € - Euro symbol (Represents €)
  • ¢ - Cent symbol (Represents ¢)
  • £ - Pound symbol (Represents £)
  • ¥ - Yen symbol (Represents ¥)
  • ≈ - Almost equal to (Represents ≈)

These characters are used in HTML to represent symbols that might otherwise be interpreted as HTML code.
For example, the ampersand (&) is used in HTML entities to represent itself, avoiding confusion with the beginning of other entities.
